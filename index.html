About Us

We Are Robo-Cat

The robotic cat project aims to revolutionize animal observation and conservation by using a bio-inspired robotic system equipped with cameras and environmental sensors. Designed to blend seamlessly into natural and domestic environments, the robotic cat provides a non-invasive means of studying animal behavior, health, and interactions. Leveraging artificial intelligence (AI) for autonomous movement and real-time data analysis, the robot can capture high-resolution imagery, monitor environmental factors like temperature and sound, and track species in their natural habitats without human interference. This innovative approach supports biodiversity conservation by providing critical insights into endangered species' behavior and ecosystem dynamics, contributing to sustainable conservation practices (SDG 15: Life on Land) and climate action (SDG 13: Climate Action). The project also enhances public engagement with environmental science, promoting education about biodiversity and sustainability (SDG 4: Quality Education). Through these capabilities, the robotic cat merges robotics, AI, and environmental research, advancing the future of wildlife monitoring and conservation.

Why Choose our project

save cats' lives

Data could help design strategies to protect endangered wildcat species or mitigate human-cat conflicts.

Non-Invasive Data Collection

Equipped with sensors and cameras, cat robots could collect data on temperature, sound, movement, and even physiological metrics like heart rate from nearby cats

Behavioral Science Advances

Observations could improve our understanding of feline cognition, communication, and adaptation

Simulation and Training

Robots can be used to simulate different scenarios and train researchers or veterinarians without needing live animals.
